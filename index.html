About

Water theft and detection system majorly looks at solving the problem that is being faced by NWSC in Uganda during the distribution of water. We are looking at designing a system that automates the process, from the anolog systems to real time systems.

Project Overview

Water theft and leakages are serious problems that many communities worldwide, including the National Water and Sewerage Corporation (NWSC), are grappling with. These challenges not only lead to water scarcity but also contribute to environmental degradation. To address this problem, Group 23-23 embarked on a project focused on using technology to tackle water theft and leakages in NWSC's water distribution network. The project involved a range of activities, including research proposal development, data collection, SRS, and development. Group 23-23 relied on several data collection methods, including conducting interviews and administering questionnaires to technicians, managers, and engineers at different branches of NWSC. The team also conducted a literature review to gain a deeper understanding of the challenges facing the organization and the factors contributing to water theft and leakages. With the data collected and analyzed, Group 23-23 was ready to move to the development phase. The team used the SRS to outline the project's requirements and ensure that the software tool met the outlined specifications. The coding and testing phase followed, ensuring that the software tool was functioning correctly before deployment. Through the development of this software tool, Group 23-23 aims to reduce water theft and leakages, thereby promoting sustainable water management practices in NWSC. As the team continues to explore innovative solutions to this challenge, they are determined to ensure that access to clean and safe water is guaranteed for all.

Proposed Topic

We narrowed down our researh topic to "Water theft and leakage detection system".

The proposed system comprises electronic, mechanical and communication systems. The electronic system comprises electronic components such as arduino boards. The mechanical part includes the water flow rate sensors, pipes and wires for demonstration. The main communication tool to be used is a GSM Module which sends data to the cloud and is retrieved using a mobile app written in flutter which will monitor the system and give feedback in real-time in the form of notifications or alerts.
