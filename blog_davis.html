Throughout our water theft and leak detection project with Group 23-23, I had the honor of conducting interviews with some of the most dedicated and experienced professionals at NWSC. As we walked into the NWSC offices, I felt a rush of excitement and anticipation about what I was going to learn from these experts and the insights they would offer about the challenges facing the organization and its water distribution network. Among the many interviews, one stood out as a truly memorable experience. It was with a technician who had been working with NWSC for over a decade, and his passion for his work was evident from the warm smile he greeted us with. As we delved into the discussion on water theft and leakages, he shared some moving stories about the negative impact of water scarcity on the local community. His urgency and conviction about addressing these challenges left me inspired and energized. I was impressed by the technician's technical expertise and his deep understanding of water distribution. He had been involved in various projects aimed at improving the network's efficiency and effectiveness. His passion for finding solutions to the organization's challenges was infectious, and I left the interview feeling motivated to tackle even the most complex problems. Despite the incredible learning opportunities, the project was not without its challenges. For me, analyzing the data we collected was daunting since I had to learn R programming from scratch. I spent long hours researching and practicing to master the language. Additionally, working on an embedded project was a new experience, and I had to learn on the job. Thankfully, my team members and supervisors provided excellent guidance and support, enabling me to make meaningful contributions to the project. We also encountered challenges during the literature review process, as it was difficult to find similar literature to inform our research. However, we persisted and identified relevant literature to guide our analysis and inform our findings. Overall, the project was an excellent opportunity to learn, grow, and contribute to a crucial issue affecting our community. It was a challenging yet rewarding experience, and I am proud of the work we accomplished as a team. I am eager to see the impact the project will have on water management practices and the community, and I am grateful for the knowledge and skills I gained from this project. Stay tuned for more updates as we move into the development of our solution.
